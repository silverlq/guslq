About me

BIO

I am a geologist and an MSc Geography student at the University of Calgary (UofC). Since my undergrad I have been interested in geospatial studies involving computer processing and complex algorithms. I am a self-taught programmer proficient in multiple programming languages, having developed several geospatial tools including knickpoint finder, a tool for applied geomorphology and neotectonics.

When not at the academy, I enjoy cycling, camping and socializing with friends especially when it involves lively sections of board games. Sporadically, I participate in game-making marathons, where I develop videogames in under 48 hours, having participated in seven such events since 2013.
